Reseller Glossary

Plain-English definitions for common reselling, marketplace, and inventory terms — alphabetical order.

B C D F L N O R S T

A

Arbitrage
The practice of buying items at a lower price from one marketplace and reselling them at a higher price on another. Common in reselling when price discrepancies exist between platforms like eBay, Poshmark, and Mercari.
Authentication
The process of verifying a user's identity before granting access to VaultLister features. VaultLister uses JWT-based authentication with optional two-factor authentication (TOTP) for enhanced security.
Average Order Value (AOV)
A key metric calculated by dividing total revenue by the number of orders. Used in VaultLister's Analytics dashboard to track sales performance over time.

B

Bundle
Two or more inventory items sold together as a single listing, typically at a combined discount. Bundles increase average order value and help move slow-moving stock alongside popular items.

C

Closet Sharing Poshmark
A Poshmark-specific action that reshares your active listings to your followers' feeds and the platform's Just In feed. Regular sharing boosts visibility and listing recency. VaultLister can automate scheduled closet shares via its Poshmark bot.
COGS Acronym Metric
The total direct cost to acquire inventory: purchase price, shipping in, authentication fees, and any prep costs. COGS is subtracted from sale price to calculate gross profit. VaultLister tracks COGS per InventoryItem so your analytics reflect true margin.
Comps (Comparables)
Recently sold listings for the same or similar item used as pricing benchmarks. Pulling comps before listing prevents both under-pricing (leaving money on the table) and over-pricing (stalling inventory). VaultLister's AI price suggestion pulls comps automatically.
Counter-Offer
A seller's response to a buyer's Offer that proposes a different price, typically between the listed price and the buyer's original offer. Counter-offers keep negotiations alive without accepting a lowball price outright.
Cross-Listing
Publishing the same InventoryItem as an active Listing on multiple marketplaces simultaneously (e.g., Poshmark, eBay, Mercari, Depop). Cross-listing maximises exposure without duplicating effort. VaultLister manages cross-listings from a single inventory record and marks items sold across all platforms when one sells.

D

Dead Stock
Inventory that has not sold within 90 or more days of listing. Dead stock ties up capital and storage space. VaultLister flags dead stock in the Inventory screen and can suggest repricing or bundling actions to clear it.
Delist
Removing an active Listing from a marketplace without marking the item as sold. Common reasons include pulling inventory to relist at a higher price, taking an item to a local sale, or seasonal storage. Delisting in VaultLister updates status across all connected platforms.

F

FBA Acronym Amazon
Fulfilled by Amazon — a fulfilment programme where sellers ship inventory to Amazon warehouses and Amazon handles storage, packing, and shipping on the seller's behalf. FBA items qualify for Prime shipping, which can significantly increase conversion.
Follow-Back
The practice of following Poshmark users who follow you, which grows your audience and increases the reach of future closet shares. VaultLister's Poshmark bot can automate follow-back within the platform's daily action limits.

L

Listing
A published record of an InventoryItem on a specific marketplace, including title, description, photos, price, and condition. A single InventoryItem can have multiple active Listings across different platforms. In VaultLister, a Listing is a distinct entity linked to its parent InventoryItem.

N

NWOT Acronym
New Without Original Tags — an item that is unused and in new condition but no longer has its original retail tags attached. NWOT typically commands a higher price than used but less than NWT.
NWT Acronym
New With Tags — an item that is unused and still has its original retail tags attached. NWT is the highest-condition designation in most resale categories and typically supports the highest price relative to comps.

O

Offer
A buyer's proposed purchase price below the listed price, submitted through a marketplace's offer system. The seller can accept, decline, or counter. In VaultLister, Offers are tracked as a distinct entity with timestamps and status, separate from the Listing price.
Offer to Likers Poshmark
A Poshmark feature that lets sellers send a private discounted price to users who have liked a listing. Offer to Likers messages expire after 24 hours. VaultLister's automation rules can trigger these offers based on like age and item age.

R

Relist
Creating a new Listing for an item that was previously listed and either expired, was deleted, or is being refreshed to appear as new on a marketplace. Relisting resets the listing date and can improve visibility on platforms that sort by recency.
ROI Acronym Metric
Return on Investment — calculated as (Sale Price − COGS − Fees) ÷ COGS × 100. A key profitability metric in VaultLister's analytics. Monitoring ROI by category or source helps identify which buying channels produce the best margins.

S

Sale
A completed transaction where a Listing is purchased by a buyer. In VaultLister, recording a Sale marks the parent InventoryItem as sold, delists it from all other platforms, and records revenue, COGS, fees, and net profit for analytics.
STR Acronym Metric
Sell-Through Rate — the percentage of listed inventory that sold within a given period, calculated as (Units Sold ÷ Units Listed) × 100. A high STR indicates strong demand or competitive pricing; a low STR suggests repricing, better photos, or a different platform may be needed.
Sourcing
The process of acquiring inventory to resell — through thrift stores, estate sales, retail arbitrage, wholesale, or online platforms. Sourcing strategy directly determines COGS and available margin.

T

Tag
A user-defined label applied to an InventoryItem for filtering, grouping, and reporting. Examples: "thrifted", "bundle-ready", "electronics", "Q2-haul". VaultLister supports multi-tag filtering across Inventory and Analytics views.
Turnaround Time
The number of days between acquiring an item and selling it. Shorter turnaround time means faster capital recycling. VaultLister calculates average turnaround time per category in the Analytics screen.
